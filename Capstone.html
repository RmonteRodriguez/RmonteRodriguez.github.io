Capstone

Overview

Summary

This was the capstone project for my college degree, spanning approximately four months. I collaborated with a team of five to develop a game inspired by an existing title. Our team chose Genshin Impact as a reference, but we put our own spin on it by adapting it into a 2D platformer to make it unique. At the end of each milestone, we held sprint reviews to showcase our progress, and I’ve included buttons to view each of these milestones. Additionally, we produced a trailer for the game to highlight its features and gameplay.

Design Goals

  • Design a genshin impact clone
  • Design levels that amplify the abilities of the character and their elemental abilities
  • Design enemies that have elemental abilities
  • Design boss enemies with unique powers compared to normal enemies

What I Worked On

Enemy Slimes

In Genshin Impact, slimes are one of the primary enemies, so we decided to include them in our game as well. I was responsible for implementing special slimes, which were designed to be stronger than the regular ones. For the larger slimes, I wanted each to have a unique ability tied to their elemental type. For example, I created an Earth Slime that would leap into the air and slam onto the ground, causing spikes to emerge and damage the player. Overall, I didn’t encounter many challenges while implementing these special slimes.

    Character Swapping

    Similar to Genshin Impact, our game features the ability to switch between characters. This was the most complex feature I implemented, as it involved a lot of moving parts. We wanted to allow the player to switch to any character, but we also needed to ensure that they couldn’t swap to a character who was already dead. Additionally, I set it up so that if a character dies, the game will automatically switch to the next available character who is still alive.

Levels/Unique Level Features

As part of the assignment, I was tasked with designing a level for our game. I created two distinct levels and followed the full design process, from moodboards and block meshes to replacing assets. Each level also featured unique elements I incorporated. In the first level, I wanted to highlight the ice character's special ability, so I designed large bodies of water that the player could only cross by freezing the water and walking across the ice. In the second level, I introduced gusts of wind that would lift the player to previously unreachable areas, utilizing our gliding feature to enhance exploration.
